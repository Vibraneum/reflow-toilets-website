## Section 2: ISO 30500 Standard Deep Dive

### Standard Overview

**ISO 30500:2018** - Non-sewered sanitation systems — Prefabricated integrated treatment units — General safety and performance requirements for design and testing

### Key Requirements

#### Safety Requirements
1. **Structural Safety**: Withstand intended loads, weather, seismic conditions
2. **Electrical Safety**: Compliance with IEC 60335-1 (household appliances)
3. **Biological Safety**: No pathogen release during normal operation
4. **Chemical Safety**: No hazardous chemical release
5. **User Safety**: Protection from moving parts, hot surfaces, sharp edges

#### Performance Requirements

**Input Parameters**:
- Design load: Specified users per day
- Input characteristics: COD, BOD, TSS, TN, pathogens

**Output Requirements**:
| Parameter | Category A | Category B |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| BOD5 | ≤10 mg/L | ≤30 mg/L |
| TSS | ≤10 mg/L | ≤30 mg/L |
| pH | 6.0-9.0 | 6.0-9.0 |
| E. coli | ≤100 CFU/100mL | ≤1000 CFU/100mL |
| Helminth eggs | <1 per liter | <1 per liter |

**B-CRT Achievement**: Category A compliant (exceeds requirements)

### Certification Process

1. **Design Review**: Documentation of treatment process
2. **Type Testing**: Laboratory validation of performance
3. **Manufacturing Audit**: Quality system verification
4. **Field Testing**: Real-world performance validation
5. **Certification Issuance**: Third-party certification body

### Certified Testing Laboratories (India)
- NEERI (National Environmental Engineering Research Institute), Nagpur
- IIT Bombay Environmental Engineering Lab
- CPCB-recognized laboratories

### B-CRT's ISO 30500 Status
- **Certification**: Achieved (through China NSFC origin technology)

## Section 6: Technical Deep Dive - Treatment Technologies Comparison

### B-CRT 8-Stage Process vs. Alternatives

#### Stage 1: Pre-Treatment (Screening)

**B-CRT Approach**:
- Automated bar screen (6mm spacing)
- Grit chamber with continuous removal
- Flow equalization

**Alternative Approaches**:
- Manual screening (labor-intensive)
- Static screens (clogging issues)
- No equalization (shock load vulnerability)

**B-CRT Advantage**: Automated, consistent performance

---

#### Stage 2: Anaerobic Digestion

**B-CRT Approach**:
- UASB (Upflow Anaerobic Sludge Blanket)
- Mesophilic temperature maintenance
- Biogas capture potential

**Alternative Approaches**:
- Septic tank (inefficient, odorous)
- Anaerobic filter (clogging)
- Imhoff tank (dated technology)

**B-CRT Advantage**: Higher efficiency, biogas recovery potential

---

#### Stage 3: Aerobic Treatment (MBBR)

**B-CRT Approach**:
- Moving Bed Biofilm Reactor
- High surface area media (500+ m²/m³)
- Fine bubble aeration
- 90%+ BOD removal

**Alternative Approaches**:
- Activated sludge (large footprint)
- Trickling filter (odor, flies)
- RBC (mechanical complexity)

**B-CRT Advantage**: Compact, efficient, low sludge production

---

#### Stage 4: Secondary Clarification

**B-CRT Approach**:
- Lamella settler (inclined plates)
- 10x footprint reduction vs conventional
- Continuous sludge withdrawal

**Alternative Approaches**:
- Conventional clarifier (large)
- Dissolved air flotation (energy-intensive)
- No clarification (poor effluent)

**B-CRT Advantage**: Compact, reliable separation

---

#### Stage 5: Ultrafiltration

**B-CRT Approach**:
- Hollow fiber UF membranes
- 0.04 micron pore size
- Automated backwash
- >99.99% pathogen removal

**Alternative Approaches**:
- Sand filtration (limited removal)
- Microfiltration (larger pores)
- No membrane (reuse not possible)

**B-CRT Advantage**: Enables water reuse, pathogen barrier

---

#### Stage 6: Advanced Oxidation (UV + H₂O₂)

**B-CRT Approach**:
- UV-C at 254nm wavelength
- 40 mJ/cm² minimum dose
- Optional H₂O₂ for AOP
- Trace organic destruction

**Alternative Approaches**:
- Chlorination (DBPs, residual)
- Ozonation (expensive, complex)
- UV only (limited for some pathogens)

**B-CRT Advantage**: Chemical-free, comprehensive disinfection

---

#### Stage 7: Activated Carbon Polishing

**B-CRT Approach**:
- Granular activated carbon (GAC)
- Removes residual organics
- Color and odor removal
- Pharmaceutical trace removal

**Alternative Approaches**:
- No polishing (limited quality)
- PAC dosing (sludge generation)
- Ion exchange (regeneration issues)

**B-CRT Advantage**: Final quality assurance

---

#### Stage 8: pH Stabilization

**B-CRT Approach**:
- Automated pH adjustment
- Calcite contactor for remineralization
- Target: 6.5-8.5 pH
- Storage-stable water

**Alternative Approaches**:
- No pH control (corrosive water)
- Chemical dosing (ongoing costs)
- Lime addition (turbidity)

**B-CRT Advantage**: Automated, low-maintenance
